Google I/O
The 2026 Dispatch

A curated reading of what Mountain View unveiled today, distilled for the Sunday Natural team. Twelve announcements that will reshape the way we build, search, and ship.

Mountain View Shoreline Amphitheatre Issue No. 001
A note from the Data team —

Every year, Google I/O sets the rhythm for the year ahead. Today's keynote was unapologetically agentic. Less about models showing off, more about quietly doing the work while you close the laptop. For a brand built on quality and intention, the takeaways are practical: shopping is becoming intelligent, search is becoming a workspace, and the line between assistant and agent has finally dissolved.

Here is what mattered, why it mattered, and where to read further.

I.

The Model Layer

Flagship

Gemini 3.5 Flash arrives, four times faster

Live today Gemini app, API, Antigravity

Google's first model in the 3.5 series is a Flash variant that, according to Sundar Pichai, outperforms Gemini 3.1 Pro on nearly all benchmarks while running roughly four times faster than competing frontier models. It is the default workhorse for agentic coding, long horizon tasks, and real world workflows. Gemini 3.5 Pro is in testing and arrives next month.

Read the keynote post →
Multimodal

Gemini Omni, any input becomes video

Omni Flash live Gemini app, Flow, YouTube Shorts

Omni is a new family of models that fuses reasoning with generation across modalities. Drop in a photo, audio clip, video, or text, and Omni outputs an edited, grounded video that respects real world physics. The first release, Omni Flash, is rolling out today to AI Plus, Pro, and Ultra subscribers worldwide.

The headline announcement

Gemini Spark
the always on agent

Spark is Google's bet on the post chat future. A personal AI agent that runs on dedicated Cloud virtual machines, twenty four hours a day, taking action across Gmail, Docs, Sheets, and third party tools via MCP. Set recurring triggers. Teach it new skills. Hand off entire workflows. Pichai said it bluntly on stage: yes, you can close your laptop. Available to AI Ultra subscribers in the US next week.

II.

Search, Reimagined

Search

AI Mode meets information agents

Summer 2026 AI Pro & Ultra subscribers

Search is now powered by Gemini 3.5 Flash, with an expanding search box that grows as your query gets longer and AI agents that monitor the web in the background for changes related to your specific question. Coming soon: Search builds custom mini apps and dashboards for your recurring tasks. Coding capabilities from Antigravity are now baked into AI Mode.

Video

Ask YouTube goes mainstream

Rolling out wider this summer

Previously exclusive to Premium, Ask YouTube turns the search bar into a conversational interface. Pose a detailed question and it returns structured answers with timestamped jumps inside videos. Useful for L&D, competitive research, and any time you want to skip the seven minute intro.

III.

Commerce & Workspace

Commerce

The Universal Cart

US, Summer 2026 Search, Gemini app, YouTube, Gmail

An agentic shopping cart that follows you across Google surfaces. Built on Google Wallet, it understands payment perks, loyalty status, and merchant offers. It tracks price history, flags compatibility issues between items, and alerts on restocks. For a DTC brand like ours, this is the most interesting infrastructure announcement of the day. Worth a closer look at the new Universal Commerce Protocol that powers it.

Universal Cart breakdown →
Workspace

Docs Live, Gmail Live, Daily Brief

Summer rollout AI Pro & Ultra, English first

Workspace gets a conversational layer. Docs Live lets you build and edit documents by voice, pulling context from Gmail, Drive, and the web. Gmail Live brings the same to inbox search. Daily Brief sifts through Gmail, Calendar, and Tasks each morning to surface what matters and suggest next steps. A Keep mode organises stream of consciousness notes into structured thought.

IV.

For the Builders

Developer

Antigravity 2.0, agent first IDE

Available globally today

Google's coding environment goes unabashedly agent first. Powered by Gemini 3.5 Flash, the 2.0 release uses multiple sub agents to plan, code, and test complex tasks in parallel. The demo had it building and running an operating system that could play Doom. The harness behind Antigravity is also what powers Spark.

Engadget liveblog →
Infrastructure

TPU 8i, the chip behind it all

Live in production

Google's eighth generation Tensor Processing Unit was the surprise hardware reveal. Faster, more energy efficient, and designed to distribute model training across global data centres. Worth tracking alongside the Google and Blackstone five billion dollar joint venture announced ahead of I/O, aimed squarely at Nvidia's dominance.

Infrastructure report →
Trust

SynthID expands, OpenAI joins

Search & Chrome

Google's invisible watermark for AI generated content is now adopted by OpenAI, Kakao, and ElevenLabs. SynthID detection rolls out to Search and Chrome alongside C2PA Content Credentials, so users can verify whether content is unedited or AI generated. A rare moment of cross industry alignment.
